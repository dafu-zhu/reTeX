\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:dispersive-intro}

In Chapter~12, we showed that waves for the one-dimensional wave equation move with a constant velocity.  However, many types of waves in physics have velocities that depend on the wave length (or equivalently, on the wave number $k$).  Such waves are called \textbf{dispersive waves}.  Examples include water waves and many types of electromagnetic waves.

In this chapter, we analyze some important physical and mathematical aspects of dispersive waves, including group velocity, wave guides, fiber optics, slowly varying waves, nonlinear wave equations (Korteweg-de~Vries and nonlinear Schr\"odinger equations), stability and instability of solutions of partial differential equations, and singular perturbation methods including the method of multiple scales and boundary layer methods.
